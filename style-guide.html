Style Guide

This guide documents the Under the Sky website as it was actually built — the plan behind it, the color palette, the styling of each main region, the layout sketch, and the license for every image used.

Project Plan

Project name Under the Sky — a personal photo journal.
Audience Hikers, stargazers, and casual travelers in the Pacific Northwest and the Southwest who like outdoor photography and are looking for places to visit.
Purpose To organize and share my own landscape and night-sky photos by theme (Stars, Hikes, Canyons), and to let visitors suggest new spots to shoot.
Personality Calm, dark, and cinematic — like standing outside at night. Cool navy and slate tones with a single warm gold accent, an elegant serif for headings, and lots of breathing room.
Comparable sites Photographer journals and gallery sites such as personal Squarespace portfolios, the Washington Trails Association photo galleries (wta.org), and national-park dark-sky pages — all clean, image-first, and easy to browse.
Features / content A home page with a featured photo and theme links; three gallery pages with responsive photo grids, captions, locations, and star ratings; a pop-up lightbox for full-size viewing.

Color Palette

The palette comes from my Mt. Rainier skyline color study: deep navy as the base, slate purple for depth, mist gray for legible text, and warm gold as the one accent. These are stored as CSS variables in format.css.

Deep Navy #0B1426 rgb(11, 20, 38) page background
Midnight Blue #1F2A44 rgb(31, 42, 68) cards / captions
Slate Purple #4A4E69 rgb(74, 78, 105) borders / muted text
Mist Gray #C9D1D9 rgb(201, 209, 217) body text
Warm Gold #E8C547 rgb(232, 197, 71) links / accents / ratings

Styling Descriptions

Overall page

The background is Deep Navy with a faint CSS starfield (layered radial gradients in layout.css) so every page feels like a night sky. Body text is Mist Gray in Karla; all headings use the Fraunces serif. Content is centered in a wrapper that maxes out at 1080 px so lines stay readable on wide screens. Styling lives in three external files: format.css (colors and type), layout.css (structure, grids, responsiveness), and effects.css (hover states and animation).

Header

The header sticks to the top of the screen as you scroll. It holds the italic Fraunces wordmark with a gold dot that gently twinkles, plus an uppercase tagline. It sits on a semi-transparent navy bar with a slate bottom border.

Navigation

The nav is a single row of text links laid out with Flexbox so it wraps on its own. Links are Mist Gray and turn Warm Gold on hover, with a gold underline that animates out from the left. The current page's link stays gold with the underline showing. On phones the header and nav stack vertically.

Main

The main area carries the page content. On the home page it opens with a full-width hero photo under a dark gradient overlay, with the title fading in on load. Galleries use a responsive CSS grid (three columns, dropping to two then one) of photo cards; each card lifts and its image zooms slightly on hover, and clicking opens a pure-CSS lightbox. Section titles are Fraunces with a gold left border.

Footer

The footer is pushed to the bottom of the screen using a flex column on the body. It has a slate top border and shows the copyright line plus a small repeat of the navigation in muted text that turns gold on hover.

Layout Sketch

Hand-drawn layouts for the three page types the site is built from — home, category (gallery), and individual photo view.
