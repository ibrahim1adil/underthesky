About

The story behind the site

I'm Ibrahim Adil, and I grew up in the Pacific Northwest, where the mountains start about thirty minutes from my front door. I got into photography because I kept seeing things on hikes and night drives that my phone couldn't quite hold on to — a sky full of stars, a moon coming up over a frozen valley — and I wanted to learn how to actually capture them.

Why this site exists. Under the Sky is my personal photo journal. It's a place to keep my favorite shots organized by what they are — stars, hikes, and canyons — instead of letting them disappear into a camera roll. If it nudges someone else to go stand outside after dark, even better.

Gear I use

  • Mirrorless camera body for low-light and night work
  • A fast wide-angle prime lens for the Milky Way
  • A lightweight travel tripod for long exposures
  • A headlamp with a red-light mode to keep my eyes adjusted to the dark
  • A phone star-map app for finding the galactic core

How often it updates

I add new photos whenever I get back from a trip and find a few keepers — usually once a month or so in summer, less in the rainy season. If you know a dark-sky spot or a trail I should shoot, the contact page is the place to tell me about it.
